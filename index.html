Cyber Ethics

Cyber Ethics refers to the moral principles and responsible behavior that guide how we use the internet, computers, and digital technology. In today’s digital age, people depend heavily on online platforms for communication, education, business, and entertainment. Our online actions can affect others. The internet provides freedom, but with that freedom comes responsibility. Cyber ethics teaches users to act with respect, protect privacy, avoid dishonest practices, and follow digital laws. It helps create a secure and positive environment for everyone.

Points about Cyber Ethics

 Respect Others Online – Be polite and do not bully, harass, or spread hate on the internet.
Protect Privacy – Never share your personal information or someone else’s details without permission.
Avoid Plagiarism – Do not copy others’ work, images, or ideas; always give credit to the original creator.
Technology Responsibly – Do not hack, spread viruses, or misuse digital tools.
Rules and Laws – Obey internet guidelines and legal rules to keep the online world safe for everyone.
